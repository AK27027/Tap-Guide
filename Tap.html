Part 2: Removing the Tap Stem

  1. Pry the cover off the faucet tap with a flat-head screwdriver.
  2. Remove the screw located under the cap, then remove the handle.
  3. Loosen the hexagonal packing nut so you can pull out the tap stem.

Part 3: Replacing the Washer and Finishing the Job

  1. Remove the screw or nut and rubber washer from the bottom of the tap stem.
  2. Take the old rubber washer to a hardware store to find a match.
  3. Place the new washer and old screw/nut onto the bottom of the tap stem.
  4. Clean off any limescale on the tap stem and tap seat with steel wool.
  5. Reinstall the tap stem, packing nut, handle, screw, and cap.
  6. Turn on the water supply and test for leaks.
